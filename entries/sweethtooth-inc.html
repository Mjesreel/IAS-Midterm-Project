Sweethtooth Inc.

Network segmentation and access control in enterprise environments.

What I Learned

Sweethtooth Inc. simulated a real enterprise network. I practiced enumeration and lateral movementâ€”moving from one compromised system to another within a network. It taught me how enterprise security works and why proper segmentation matters.

Network Enumeration

Enumeration is the process of gathering information about a network. I learned to:

  • Scan for live hosts and open ports
  • Identify running services and their versions
  • Map out network topology
  • Discover user accounts and groups
  • Find shared resources like file servers

Lateral Movement

Once I gained access to one system, I needed to move laterally to reach other systems. This involved:

  • Using compromised credentials on other machines
  • Exploiting trust relationships between systems
  • Pivoting through compromised hosts to reach isolated networks
  • Escalating privileges to gain administrative access

Network Segmentation

I learned why network segmentation is so important. Segmentation means dividing a network into smaller, isolated sections. This limits how far an attacker can move if they compromise one system.

For example:

  • Separate networks for different departments
  • Isolated networks for sensitive systems like databases
  • DMZ (demilitarized zone) for public-facing servers
  • Strict firewall rules between segments

Access Control

Proper access control means ensuring users only have access to what they need. I learned about:

  • Principle of least privilege: Give minimum necessary permissions
  • Role-based access control: Permissions based on job roles
  • Multi-factor authentication: Requiring multiple forms of verification
  • Regular access reviews: Checking who has access to what

Why This Matters

Enterprise networks are complex, with hundreds or thousands of systems. Without proper segmentation and access control, one compromised account can lead to a complete breach. This challenge showed me how real corporate security works and what defenders need to implement.

"In enterprise security, containment is everything. Segment your networks, control access, and limit the blast radius of any breach."
